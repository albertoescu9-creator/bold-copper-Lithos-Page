Core Palette · Copper as Primary Accent

Copper #A0614A replaces Gold as the primary accent. It brings earthy warmth rooted in geological material — terracotta, oxidised rock, clay seams. The hue sits between the Warm Brown dark and the Rose Grey mid-tone, creating a palette that feels cohesive and distinctively grounded. All other anchors remain: Stone, Cream, Rose Grey, White.

Where Copper Should NOT Be Used
Long body text in Copper
Don't
Body text in Copper
Copper lacks sufficient contrast for extended reading. Use Warm Brown for body text on light and Cream on dark. Copper is for accents only.
LARGE FILL AREA
Don't
Large background fills
Copper should not fill entire sections or hero backgrounds. It works as accent bars (3px), badges, and highlights — not as a dominant surface colour.

Do
CTAs and action elements
Copper excels as button backgrounds, active borders, hover states, and 3px accent bars. High-impact, small surface area.

Do
Eyebrows, labels, tags
Use Copper Deep (#7A4A38) for text on white. Use Copper Light (#C4856E) for labels on dark. These maintain readability.
